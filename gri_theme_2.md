Create a high-end streetwear curation web UI with a "Raw Editorial" aesthetic. Move away from tech-heavy UI; focus on analog print and high-fashion magazine layouts.

Visual Identity:

Color Palette: Off-white paper texture (#F5F5F5) as the primary background, deep charcoal (#1A1A1A) for text, and "Safety Orange" or "Deep Burgundy" as a minimal accent for status tags.

Typography: A sophisticated mix. Use a high-contrast Serif font (like Didot or Bodoni) for editorial headlines to give a premium feel, paired with a heavy-weight Sans-Serif (like Akzidenz-Grotesk) for brand names and prices.

Texture: Subtle paper grain and scan-line textures. Images should look like they are 35mm film scans with natural grain and slight motion blur.

Layout & Elements:

Grid System: Asymmetrical "Swiss Style" grid. Break the grid occasionally—let images overlap text or bleed off the edges of the screen.

Product/Brand Cards: No borders. Use large, raw street-style photography. Instead of traditional buttons, use underlined text links or "scanned" sticker-style tags for discounts (e.g., a "30% OFF" tag that looks like a physical price sticker).

Navigation: Vertical navigation on the far left or right, styled like a magazine's table of contents.

Atmosphere: Raw, authentic, high-fashion, minimalist but textured. The feeling of an underground zine found in a Tokyo backstreet.
